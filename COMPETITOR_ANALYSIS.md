## MeQ Areas of Strength

### 1. Framework flexibility (industry-leading)
MeQ is the **only product** in this sample that lets schools define their own custom domains, questions, scoring thresholds, and interventions end-to-end. Most competitors use a single fixed framework (PASS's 9 factors, SELweb's competencies, DESSA's strengths model). Bounce Together offers a library of 65 pre-built surveys but schools cannot easily build their own bespoke framework with custom scoring.

### 2. Dual-lens assessment
MeQ captures both student self-report AND teacher observation for the same child. Only DESSA and Satchel Pulse offer teacher-rated assessments in this sample, and neither combines them with student self-report the way MeQ does.

### 3. Reliability scoring
MeQ's validation-pair + trap-question reliability system is unusual. PASS and DESSA have statistical validity. Most competitors have no reliability check at all.

### 4. Reduced question mode + tiered design
Junior (5-7) and Standard (8-11) tiers with reduced mode option gives schools flexibility that few competitors match.

### 5. CSV question import + builder UI
Super-admin framework builder with CSV bulk upload is unique in this sample. Most products are "closed box" with fixed questions.

### 6. Multi-campus admin
Admins managing multiple schools with a single login тАФ no competitor in the sample matches this.

### 7. Term-over-term progress tracking + per-domain scoring JSON
Queryable per-domain historical data with change deltas between assessments.

### 8. Pulse + full assessment in one platform
Komodo and Closegap do pulse. Bounce/PASS/DESSA do full assessments. **MeQ does both** тАФ with the pulse linked to the same framework domains as the assessment.

## MeQ Gaps vs Competitors

### Critical gaps (competitors consistently have these)

**1. CPOMS and MIS integrations**
Every UK competitor integrates with CPOMS (safeguarding) and MIS (SIMS/Arbor/Bromcom/Wonde). This is table stakes for UK schools and MeQ has neither. **High priority.**

**2. Google / Microsoft SSO**
Steer, Bounce, Panorama, Satchel, Closegap, DESSA, SELweb all support SSO. MeQ uses email/password only. Schools hate managing separate credentials. **High priority.**

**3. National benchmarking**
PASS has 600k student benchmarks. Bounce Together has 12 million responses. This is a massive credibility signal MeQ lacks. Hard to build without volume, but schools ask for it. **Medium priority тАФ volume-dependent.**

**4. Bespoke / one-off surveys**
Komodo, Bounce, Panorama, Satchel all offer custom survey builders. MeQ has this in the backlog (Task #24).

**5. Tiered (MTSS) intervention support**
Panorama, Satchel, DESSA, PASS all tie interventions to tier 1 (universal) / tier 2 (targeted) / tier 3 (individual) support. MeQ has a flat intervention bank per level but no tiering logic.

### Meaningful gaps

**6. Safeguarding flagging and alerts**
Komodo, Steer, Bounce, Panorama, Satchel, Closegap all flag concerning responses in real-time with email/dashboard alerts. MeQ has a basic flagged list on the Pulse dashboard but no alerting.

**7. Multilingual interface**
PASS offers 27 languages. Panorama and DESSA support multiple languages. MeQ is English only. Critical for international schools (including your UAE market).

**8. Audio read-aloud actually built**
SELweb and Closegap have this live. MeQ has the `audioUrl` field in the schema but no upload UI and no audio files. Task #23.

**9. Anonymous response option**
Critical for sensitive surveys (bullying, mental health). Panorama, Satchel, and Bounce all support it. Needed for Task #24.

**10. Impact tracking on interventions**
Steer, Bounce, Panorama, Satchel, DESSA all let schools mark an intervention as applied and track whether scores improved afterwards. MeQ doesn't track this.

### Nice-to-have gaps

**11. Student-initiated check-ins**
Komodo, YouHQ and Closegap let students proactively request help. MeQ is always scheduled/assigned.

**12. Parent/family surveys**
Panorama is strong here. MeQ has no parent-facing tooling.

**13. AI-driven insights**
Panorama (Solara), Bounce (AI recommendations), Satchel all market AI analysis. MeQ has no AI layer.

**14. Staff wellbeing surveys**
**YouHQ** and Satchel Pulse both measure staff wellbeing alongside students. Panorama supports staff surveys too. MeQ only measures students.

**15. Self-led student wellbeing content**
**YouHQ** bundles CBT-style modules, Wellbeing Warrior activities, and relaxation content. Closegap has in-product coping activities. MeQ has no student-facing wellbeing content тАФ it's an assessment tool only.

**16. In-app support messaging**
**YouHQ** lets students message trusted staff in-app. MeQ has no communication layer.

**17. Training and implementation services**
**YouHQ** bundles live training and workshops. Bounce Together provides webinars. MeQ is software-only.

**18. Crisis/self-harm keyword detection**
None of the products clearly explain this, but it's implied safeguarding territory тАФ you flagged it for free-text moderation in Task #24.

## Strategic Recommendations

### Quick wins (daysтАУweeks)
1. **Google/Microsoft SSO** тАФ well-trodden implementation with NextAuth. Biggest unlock for UK school adoption.
2. **CPOMS integration** тАФ reach out to CPOMS for their integration spec. Even basic "concerning response тЖТ CPOMS incident" is competitive.
3. **Anonymous surveys** тАФ goes in Task #24. Low effort, high value.
4. **Real-time safeguarding alerts** тАФ email DSL when pulse score or free text flags concern. Enhances existing flagged student feature.

### Medium term (weeks)
5. **Wire up audio + image uploads** (Task #23)
6. **Bespoke surveys with templates** (Task #24)
7. **Intervention impact tracking** тАФ simple "did scores improve after intervention X was applied?"
8. **Multilingual support** тАФ at minimum Arabic for UAE, French, Spanish. Next-intl library.
9. **MIS integration via Wonde** тАФ Wonde is the UK standard abstraction layer; integrate once and get SIMS/Arbor/Bromcom for free.

### Longer term (months)
10. **MTSS tier 1/2/3 intervention model** тАФ reshapes the interventions tab to show tier-appropriate strategies.
11. **National benchmarking** тАФ wait until you have ~100 schools of data, then publish comparison reports.
12. **Parent-facing surveys** тАФ extension of Task #24 survey system.
13. **AI insights** тАФ once you have enough data, summarise trends with a Claude API call at assessment completion.

## Positioning

MeQ's unique position is **framework flexibility + dual-lens + pulse in one tool**. No competitor combines all three. Your pitch should be:

> "The only wellbeing platform that lets schools build their own SEL framework тАФ from bespoke domains and questions through to matching interventions тАФ alongside both a termly deep-dive and a weekly pulse check-in, all validated with reliability scoring."

The framework builder is genuinely differentiating. Lean into it.

### vs YouHQ specifically (the closest UK competitor)

YouHQ's pitch is essentially "**PERMAH wellbeing in a box**, with content, training, and safeguarding." It's strong for schools that want an opinionated, pre-packaged approach with lots of hand-holding. MeQ's pitch should be "**bring your own framework**, with psychometric rigour and dual-lens assessment." Different customers:

- YouHQ wins schools that want to be told what to measure and get content to use
- MeQ wins schools (or trusts, or international schools) that already have a framework or want to build one specific to their context

**But MeQ can't win on framework flexibility alone if it lacks CPOMS + MIS integration.** Those are table stakes тАФ no UK school will adopt a tool that forces manual student roster management and can't log safeguarding concerns to their existing system. These need to be built before any serious sales push into UK schools.

## YouHQ тАФ Closer Look

**YouHQ (youhq.co.uk)** is the most directly comparable UK competitor to MeQ. Based on live research:

- **Framework**: Built on the **PERMAH model** (Positive Emotion, Engagement, Relationships, Meaning, Accomplishment, Health) тАФ the same model MeQ seeded as one of its example frameworks. Developed with psychologists and character education experts. **Fixed framework тАФ no school customisation of the model itself.**
- **Audience**: Both students AND staff тАФ one of the few tools that measures both populations
- **Content library**: Wellbeing framework bundled with:
  - **"Wellbeing Warrior" activities** organised by PERMAH domain
  - Bonus wellbeing resources
  - "Relax" content for calming
  - **Self-led student modules** with teacher guides
  - CBT-style resources for pastoral intervention
- **Pulse/check-ins**: Mood check-ins with automatic low-mood alerts to staff
- **Safeguarding** тАФ "APPIA methodology" with 5 components ending in "Alert":
  - Direct integration with **CPOMS** and **MyConcern**
  - Teachers can push student profiles directly to safeguarding specialists
  - For schools without CPOMS/MyConcern, an internal designated safeguarding committee option
  - Aligned to KCSIE (Keeping Children Safe in Education)
- **MIS integration**: Via **Wonde** тАФ auto-syncs student/staff data
- **Reporting**: Snapshot reports, FOCUS feature highlights students needing more support, automatic low mood notifications, in-app support messaging
- **Training**: Sells live training, interactive workshops, and offline resources alongside the platform
- **Pricing**: Per-user annual subscription with tiered plans:
  - **Essentials** тАФ core surveys, reporting, MIS integration, Learning Hub, early alerts
  - **Higher tiers** тАФ student values-based goal setting, journalling, integration with other school apps

### What YouHQ does that MeQ doesn't

1. **Staff wellbeing surveys** тАФ YouHQ explicitly covers staff as well as students
2. **Self-led student wellbeing modules** тАФ CBT-style content, mindfulness, PERMAH-organised activities
3. **CPOMS + MyConcern integration** тАФ direct push of student profiles to safeguarding systems
4. **Wonde MIS integration** тАФ automatic student roster sync
5. **Student-initiated mood check-ins** тАФ students can proactively check in
6. **In-app support messaging** тАФ secure messages between students and support staff
7. **KCSIE-aligned safeguarding flow** тАФ structured methodology for safeguarding escalation
8. **Bundled training and workshops** тАФ not just software, but implementation support

### What MeQ does that YouHQ doesn't

1. **Custom frameworks** тАФ YouHQ is locked to PERMAH; MeQ lets schools build their own
2. **Multiple pre-built frameworks** тАФ MeQ ships with MeQ Standard, HEART, PERMA, Character Education
3. **CSV question bulk upload**
4. **Teacher-rated assessments** (dual-lens student + teacher view of same child)
5. **Reliability scoring** with validation pairs and trap questions
6. **Multi-campus admin login**
7. **Reduced question mode**
8. **Custom scoring thresholds per framework**

**Bottom line on YouHQ**: It's MeQ's most direct competitor in UK market. Stronger on safeguarding integrations, staff wellbeing, and content library. Weaker on framework flexibility and psychometric rigour. MeQ's differentiation is clear: **YouHQ is a pre-packaged PERMAH platform; MeQ is a flexible assessment toolkit**. Schools that want PERMAH + safeguarding + CBT content pick YouHQ. Schools that want to define their own framework pick MeQ тАФ if MeQ can close the CPOMS/MIS gap.

## Additional Product Detail (from training data тАФ verify before use)

The following adds colour that wasn't visible on marketing homepages.

### Panorama Education
- Modular тАФ schools pick topics (Grit, Growth Mindset, Self-Efficacy, Sense of Belonging, Teacher-Student Relationships)
- Typical survey ~20-40 items, 1-3x/year
- **Panorama "Playbook"** тАФ extensive intervention library with thousands of tagged strategies auto-mapped to low-scoring competencies
- **Panorama Student Success** warehouse merges SEL + attendance + behaviour + academic data
- National benchmark norms across demographics
- Full MTSS tier 1/2/3 with screeners, progress monitoring, intervention tracking, fidelity

### Satchel Pulse
- CASEL's 5 competencies (~25 items per assessment)
- **Every ~6 weeks** тАФ half-termly cadence (notable, and matches UK term structure)
- Class heatmaps, auto-matched strategies library
- Part of Satchel/Show My Homework ecosystem тАФ one of the few with **genuine UK presence** among US SEL products

### SELweb (xSEL Labs)
- **Direct performance assessment** (not self-report) тАФ the only major product doing this
- Built by Clark McKown at Rush University
- Interactive tasks: emotion recognition, social problem-solving vignettes, self-control mini-tasks
- Takes ~30-45 minutes, 1-2x/year
- K-3 (Early Elementary) and 4-6 (Late Elementary) versions
- Arguably more defensible for young children and less gameable than self-report
- **Standardised, no customisation** тАФ sells as a psychometric instrument

### DESSA (Aperture Education)
- **8 competencies**: Self-Awareness, Self-Management, Social-Awareness, Relationship Skills, Goal-Directed Behavior, Personal Responsibility, Decision Making, Optimistic Thinking
- **DESSA-mini** (8 items) for rapid screening, **DESSA** (72 items) for full assessment, **DESSA-HSE** (high school)
- Completed primarily by **teachers rating students** (strengths-based) тАФ student self-report is secondary
- Typical MTSS cadence: mini 3x/year, full on flagged students only
- **Strengths-based framing** is the signature тАФ rare in this market
- Aperture "Evo" platform for district dashboards
- Nationally normed

### Second Step (Committee for Children)
- **Primarily a curriculum**, not an assessment tool
- K-8 teacher-led lessons + digital content
- Separate "Second Step SEL Check-In" (~10 items, student self-report)
- Huge US elementary market penetration
- Bundled Bullying Prevention and Child Protection units
- Non-profit credibility signal

### Closegap
- **Daily** emotional check-in тАФ student picks from feeling wheel, rates energy, short questions
- **Under 2 minutes**, done daily or on-demand
- Primarily K-5 elementary
- Live classroom view showing who's checked in and how
- Auto-flags concerning responses
- In-product regulation activities (breathing, movement, journaling) students do immediately after check-in
- **Free to schools** (non-profit funded)
- Closer to a mental health companion than an assessment

## Broader Market Observations (US vs UK patterns)

1. **MTSS is the US operating system.** Tier 1/2/3 screening тЖТ diagnostic тЖТ progress monitoring тЖТ fidelity tracking is baked into every US product. UK products don't speak this language тАФ graduated response is the rough equivalent but isn't architected into tooling. **Opportunity:** UK schools moving into MAT structures are MTSS-curious, and explicitly supporting this would open US doors too.

2. **Demographic disaggregation** тАФ US products make subgroup comparison (race, SES, EL status, IEP) first-class features driven by federal reporting. UK tools rarely disaggregate by FSM, EAL, SEND status. MeQ has SEN marking but doesn't yet aggregate reports by it.

3. **Frequency spread** тАФ Daily (Closegap) / 6-weekly (Satchel Pulse) / termly (MeQ, PASS, Steer) / annual (Panorama) all exist. Daily is least saturated, especially in the UK. MeQ's Weekly Pulse sits in a sweet spot competitors don't quite occupy.

4. **Longitudinal student success warehouses** тАФ Panorama's big play is merging SEL with attendance, behaviour and academic data as one early-warning system. UK pastoral-data tools (CPOMS, MyConcern, Class Charts) are siloed. **A UK product unifying SEL + pastoral logs + attendance would be distinctive domestically.**

5. **Intervention libraries wired to low scores** тАФ Panorama Playbook, DESSA Growing SEL library, Satchel strategies all auto-recommend activities based on weakest competency. MeQ has the mechanism (interventions by domain/level) тАФ opportunity to make it feel more "suggested to you" rather than "here's a list."

6. **Clever / ClassLink rostering** тАФ US SSO standard, centralised around these two vendors. UK equivalents (Wonde, Groupcall Xporter) don't have US analogues. Wonde is the right first integration for UK; Clever/ClassLink would be needed for any US push.
